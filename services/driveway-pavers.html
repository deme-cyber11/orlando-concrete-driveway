Pavers vs. Concrete: Which Is Right for Your Driveway?

Choosing between pavers and traditional concrete is one of the most important decisions you'll make for your driveway. Here's an honest comparison to help you decide:

Advantages of Pavers

  • Easy Repairs: Replace individual pavers without visible patches
  • Flexibility: Interlock design resists cracking from ground movement
  • Design Options: Unlimited colors, patterns, and textures
  • Drainage: Permeable options available for better water management
  • Durability: Can withstand heavier loads than standard concrete
  • Property Value: Premium appearance increases home value
  • Instant Use: Can drive on immediately after installation

Advantages of Concrete

  • Lower Cost: 30-50% less expensive than pavers
  • Less Maintenance: No joint sand to replace or weeds to manage
  • Smooth Surface: Easier for snow removal (not a major factor in Orlando)
  • Quick Installation: Faster to install than pavers
  • Modern Look: Clean, contemporary appearance
  • Stamped Options: Can mimic paver look at lower cost

Our Recommendation

For Orlando homeowners prioritizing curb appeal and long-term value, we typically recommend pavers. For budget-conscious projects or homes with a modern aesthetic, traditional concrete or stamped concrete may be better choices.

Popular Paver Patterns for Driveways

The pattern you choose affects both the appearance and structural integrity of your paver driveway. Here are the most popular options:

Herringbone (45 or 90 Degree)

Best for Driveways - Recommended

The herringbone pattern is the gold standard for driveway paver installations. The interlocking zigzag arrangement creates superior structural integrity that resists shifting and spreading under vehicle weight. The 45-degree angle is traditional, while 90-degree offers a more modern look.

Running Bond (Staggered)

Classic & Versatile

Similar to traditional brick walls, this offset pattern creates a clean, classic look. It's slightly less structurally stable than herringbone but offers a timeless appearance that works with any architectural style. Best used with a strong edge restraint system.

Basket Weave

Traditional Elegance

Pairs of pavers are arranged in alternating horizontal and vertical groups, creating a woven appearance. This pattern adds visual interest while maintaining good structural stability. Perfect for colonial and traditional homes.

Circular/Fan Pattern

Dramatic Focal Point

Pavers radiate outward from a central point, creating a stunning visual effect. Often used for circular driveways or as an accent at the driveway entrance. Requires skilled installation and typically costs more due to increased labor.

Random/Ashlar Pattern

Natural Stone Look

Multiple paver sizes are combined to create an organic, natural appearance similar to flagstone. This pattern works exceptionally well with travertine and concrete pavers designed to mimic natural stone.

Paver Driveway Installation Process

Our professional paver installation follows a proven process that ensures your driveway will look beautiful and perform flawlessly for decades:

1

Design Consultation & Site Evaluation

We assess your property, discuss paver options, review patterns and colors, and provide a detailed quote. We'll consider drainage, existing landscaping, and your home's architecture to recommend the best solution.

2

Excavation & Grading

We excavate to the proper depth (typically 8-12 inches) and establish the correct slope for water drainage away from your home. Old concrete or asphalt is removed if replacing an existing driveway.

3

Base Preparation

We install a compacted crushed stone base (typically 4-6 inches) that provides the stable foundation your pavers need. This is the most critical step - a poorly prepared base leads to settling and shifting.

4

Edge Restraint Installation

Heavy-duty edge restraints are installed around the perimeter to prevent pavers from spreading outward over time. We use commercial-grade restraints anchored with steel spikes.

5

Sand Bedding Layer

A 1-inch layer of coarse bedding sand is screeded to provide a smooth, level surface for paver placement. This layer allows for minor adjustments during installation.

6

Paver Installation

Pavers are carefully laid according to your chosen pattern. Each paver is placed by hand to ensure proper spacing and alignment. Border pavers and cuts are made with precision saws.

7

Compaction & Joint Sand

A plate compactor is used to settle pavers into the bedding sand and ensure a level surface. Polymeric sand is then swept into the joints - this special sand hardens when wet, locking pavers together and preventing weed growth.

8

Sealing (Optional)

We recommend sealing your new paver driveway to enhance color, protect against stains, and make maintenance easier. Sealing is included at no extra cost with our premium packages.

Durability in Florida's Climate

Orlando's climate presents unique challenges for outdoor surfaces. Here's why pavers excel in our environment:

  • Heat Resistance: Quality pavers (especially travertine) absorb less heat than dark asphalt and stay cooler underfoot
  • UV Stability: Integral colors in concrete pavers resist fading from intense Florida sun
  • Heavy Rain: Proper grading and permeable options handle tropical downpours effectively
  • Ground Movement: Flexible paver systems adapt to Florida's sandy, shifting soils without cracking
  • Hurricane Debris: Individual damaged pavers can be easily replaced after severe weather
  • Salt & Chemical Resistance: Quality pavers resist damage from pool chemicals, fertilizers, and road treatments
  • No Freeze-Thaw Issues: While not a concern in Orlando, pavers are ideal for vacation home owners from northern states

Paver Driveway Maintenance

One of the biggest advantages of paver driveways is straightforward maintenance. Here's what to expect:

Regular Maintenance (Homeowner Tasks)

  • Weekly: Sweep or blow off debris to prevent staining and organic buildup
  • Monthly: Rinse with garden hose to remove dirt and pollen
  • As Needed: Spot-treat stains from oil, grease, or rust with appropriate cleaners
  • Annually: Inspect for any shifted pavers, weed growth, or ant hills in joints

Periodic Professional Maintenance

  • Every 2-3 Years: Re-sand joints with polymeric sand as needed
  • Every 3-5 Years: Professional power washing and re-sealing (recommended but optional)
  • As Needed: Replace any cracked or damaged individual pavers

Maintenance Advantage: Unlike poured concrete, damaged pavers can be individually replaced without visible patches. We stock common paver styles and can often match older installations.

Frequently Asked Questions About Driveway Pavers

How long do driveway pavers last in Orlando?

Quality driveway pavers can last 25-50+ years in Orlando's climate when properly installed and maintained. Concrete pavers and brick pavers are extremely durable, while travertine may require slightly more maintenance but offers exceptional beauty and longevity.

How much do driveway pavers cost in Orlando?

Driveway paver installation in Orlando typically costs $15-$30 per square foot, depending on the paver type and pattern complexity. A typical 400-600 square foot driveway ranges from $6,000-$18,000 fully installed.

Are pavers better than concrete for driveways?

Pavers offer several advantages over poured concrete: they're easier to repair (just replace individual pavers), more flexible (resist cracking from Florida's soil movement), offer better drainage options, and provide superior curb appeal. However, concrete is more affordable upfront. Compare our concrete driveway installation options.

What is the best paver pattern for driveways?

Herringbone is the strongest pattern for driveways because it creates an interlocking structure that distributes vehicle weight evenly and prevents shifting. Running bond and basket weave are also excellent choices for their classic appearance and structural integrity.

Do paver driveways need maintenance?

Paver driveways require periodic maintenance including re-sanding joints every 2-3 years, optional sealing every 3-5 years, removing weeds if they appear, and occasional power washing. However, maintenance is straightforward and repairs are simple compared to concrete.

Can you install pavers over existing concrete?

Yes, in some cases pavers can be installed over existing concrete if the surface is in good condition and drainage allows. This can reduce costs by eliminating demolition. We'll assess your existing driveway during the consultation.

How long does paver driveway installation take?

Most residential paver driveway installations take 3-5 days depending on size and complexity. This includes excavation, base preparation, paver laying, and finishing. Unlike concrete, you can drive on pavers immediately after installation.

Will weeds grow between my pavers?

We use polymeric sand in all paver joints, which hardens and significantly reduces weed growth. With proper sealing and occasional maintenance, weeds are rarely a significant issue. Any that do appear can be easily treated or pulled.
